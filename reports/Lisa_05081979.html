New Strategy

Here's what works instead, babe. You step back and let people be their own authority. 3. Monthly equality check-in. Last Sunday of every month, journal on this prompt: "Where did I treat someone as less capable than me this month? What would change if I saw them as my equal?" (6) The Lovers

Your 6 (The Lovers) asks you to choose what you actually want — not what looks good on paper, not what keeps the peace, but what makes your body say yes. This is your daily litmus test for every choice: love it or leave it. No liminal half-yeses. The practices that activate this energy: ruthless calendar edits, saying no without a reason, choosing the meal you want instead of the one you think you should order, noticing which people make you feel alive and prioritising them. The contexts: moments where you have actual agency but you're pretending you don't. Small daily choices train the muscle for the big ones. The false-comfort version you keep slipping into is consensus-seeking — polling everyone else's opinion until yours dissolves, staying in situations that are fine-but-not-quite because leaving feels rude. Lisa, the 6 doesn't do committees. It does clarity. You already know what you want. Stop asking permission to want it.

(5) The Hierophant

Your 5 (The Hierophant) guides you to become the authority you kept waiting for. This is the energy of mastery through lineage — study, apprenticeship, teaching what you know. It activates when you commit to a formal learning path, when you mentor someone else, when you stop improvising and build a real system. The people: teachers who demand rigour, traditions that have stood the test, communities with standards. The contexts: anywhere you're asked to step into the elder role, to pass knowledge down the line, to honour what works instead of reinventing everything from scratch. The shadow slip is playing student forever — another course, another certification, another book before you're ready to speak. Or worse: dismissing all structure as oppressive and staying scrappy out of fear that real mastery would box you in. The Hierophant says the opposite. Structure is freedom. Mastery is what lets you break the rules with precision. Stop waiting for someone to anoint you. You've been the expert for a while now.

(8) Justice

Your 8 (Justice) is the practice of cause-and-effect thinking applied to your own life. It guides you to name the truth without decoration — what you did, what happened, what you owe, what's owed to you. This energy activates in moments of brutal honesty: journaling without spin, clearing debts (financial and emotional), holding yourself and others to the same standard, saying the hard thing because it's accurate. The people who activate it: those who call you on your contradictions without cruelty, anyone who models integrity over likability. The contexts: contract negotiations, apologies that matter, moments when you have to choose between comfortable and correct. The false version is cold scorecard logic — tit-for-tat fairness with no room for mercy, or flipping to the other extreme and never holding anyone accountable because you're afraid of being the bad guy. Justice isn't punitive. It's just clear-eyed. You know when the scales are off. Stop pretending they're not.
