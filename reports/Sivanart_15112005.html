2. The no-test rule. For the next 90 days, when the urge to test someone's affection surfaces (withdrawing, creating tension, engineering jealousy), name it out loud to a trusted friend or write it down instead of acting on it. Then do the opposite: be direct. Say what you need. Ask the question you were going to manipulate them into answering. It will feel terrifying. It works.

3. Desire audit. Once a week, sit down and ask yourself: *What do I actually want right now in love — not what I think I should want, not what keeps them interested, but what I want.* Write it out. Don't edit for palatability. This recalibrates your inner compass. The 12 at centre demands you stop performing and start *feeling* — this practice is the bridge.
