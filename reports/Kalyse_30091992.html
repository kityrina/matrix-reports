Love without the spell. The new deal is transparency — walking into the room as yourself and letting people respond to *that*. "I'd like to leave now." "I need you to listen without fixing." Notice the impulse to smooth it over mid-sentence and don't. 2. Weekly mirror work with the 18. Sit with your reflection for ten minutes every Sunday. No makeup, no performance. Say out loud: "This is the face that gets loved. Not the one I show them." Let yourself cry if the 18 brings up fear or shame. The Moon holds the truth under the illusion — you're learning to live there.

3. Audit one relationship for the bargain. Pick someone close — friend, lover, colleague. Write down: What do I give them? What do I get back? Is the exchange clean, or am I performing to keep them? If it's performance, have one conversation where you show up flat — no charm, no dazzle, just honesty. See if they stay. That's the data you need.

The 6 (The Lovers) on your sacral is pleasure as medicine — and repression as poison. This chakra governs sexuality, creativity, the womb, the lower back, the hips. It wants to *choose* what it desires and then claim it fully. When you override your yes or no to please someone else, the sacral registers betrayal: lower back pain, menstrual irregularities, hip tension, creative blocks, libido that flatlines. Your karmic tail (18-6-6, Love Magic) touches this channel — past-life manipulation through beauty and charm left a residue of guilt around your own magnetism. This lifetime, the lesson is clean desire. No games, no dimming, no using sex as currency. What clears it: moving your hips (dance, yoga, anything fluid), honoring your cycle, and choosing partners who meet your full want — not your fear of being too much.

Root (Muladhara) · 21 — The World

Body systems: Urogenital system, lower limbs, large intestine, tailbone, sacrum, legs.

The 21 (The World) on your root chakra is completion energy at the foundation — mastery, global reach, integrated wholeness. When this channel is clear, you feel *held* by life itself. Grounded, safe, able to build. The shadow: premature satisfaction or plateau. You stop before the final mile because you've hit "good enough." Root issues show as: lower body weakness, chronic fatigue, immune system crashes, feeling unmoored or like you're floating through life without traction. The 21 asks you to finish what you start and trust that the earth will hold the weight of your full arrival. What supports it: bare feet on ground, heavy weight-bearing movement (squats, walking with purpose), and completing one thing fully before starting the next. Your root doesn't fear the work — it fears you'll abandon the throne before you sit in it.
