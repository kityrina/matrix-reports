New Strategy

Here's what works instead, babe: equal footing. Not because people deserve your validation, but because hierarchy kills intimacy and intimacy is where your 6 (The Lovers) centre actually lives. The new pattern is lateral relating — curiosity instead of assessment, questions instead of corrections. You stop volunteering your two cents and start listening past the first sentence. When you catch yourself about to fix someone, you pause and ask what they need instead. This isn't dimming your brilliance; it's changing the altitude. Your Hermit wisdom becomes mentorship, not monarchy. Your standards stay high, but you apply them to yourself first. The kicker: once you stop needing to be right, your 21 mastery becomes magnetic instead of isolating. People want to be around you because you see them, not through them.

Steps to Take

1. **The 10-Second Pause.** Every time you're about to offer advice, insight, or correction, count to ten in silence first. Then ask, "Do you want my take on this?" and honour the answer. If they say no, sit in the discomfort of not being the expert in the room. Track how often you bypass this step — that's your Overseer reflex in action.

2. **Weekly Vulnerability Practice.** Once a week, tell someone something you don't know or something you need help with. Text a friend: "I'm struggling with X — do you have insight?" or "I don't understand Y — can you explain?" Let them be the knower. Your nervous system will hate this at first. Do it anyway. It rewires the superiority default.

3. **Reflection Journaling: Where I Elevated Today.** Every evening, write down one moment you positioned yourself above someone else — in tone, word choice, or silence. No shame, just noticing. Then rewrite the moment as if you were equals in the room. Over time, this builds the muscle of horizontal relating before the conversation even starts.
