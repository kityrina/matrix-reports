New Strategy

The shift is from *knowing for them* to *witnessing with them.* You stop solving, stop steering, stop subtly positioning yourself above. Instead: radical equality. Steps to Take

1. Practice the Pause-and-Ask. In any conversation where you feel the urge to advise, correct, or organise — pause for three full breaths and ask an open question instead. "What do you think you'll do?" or "How does that feel for you?" No follow-up fix. Just the question. Let them sit in their own process. This rewires the Overseer reflex at the micro level and teaches your nervous system that people don't need you to have the answer.

2. Weekly Equality Audit. Every Sunday, write down three moments from the past week where you positioned yourself as superior — even subtly. Name the dynamic (who, what happened, what you were protecting by staying 'above'). Then rewrite it: what would the peer-level version have looked like? This isn't about shame. It's about pattern recognition. You can't shift what you don't see.

3. Receive without reciprocating. Let someone help you with something this week — and do not offer anything in return, do not 'even the score,' do not turn it into a teaching moment about their generosity. Just say thank you and sit in the discomfort of being on the receiving end. The Overseer hates this. Do it anyway. True equality requires you to need people, not just bestow upon them.
