New Strategy

Here's what works instead, babe: trade the courtroom for curiosity. The new strategy is witness, not judge. You stop asking "Is this fair?" and start asking "What's really happening here?" The Emperor (your centre) doesn't need Justice (your talent) to weaponize itself — it needs structure that protects without punishing. Steps to Take

1. **The Resentment Inventory — weekly purge.** Every Sunday, write down three things you're keeping score on. Name the person, the grievance, the imagined debt. Then ask: "What would change if I forgave this without requiring proof?" Burn the list. Literally. You're not forgiving them for their sake — you're releasing yourself from the exhausting job of cosmic accountant.

2. **Interrupt the courtroom voice with The Work by Byron Katie.** When you catch yourself building a case against someone ("She should have…" / "He always…"), run it through the four questions: Is it true? Can I absolutely know it's true? Who am I with that thought? Who would I be without it? This rewires the automatic prosecution reflex into curiosity.

3. **Practice one act of unmeasured generosity per week.** Give something — time, money, attention — with zero expectation of return. Not to prove you're evolved. To teach your nervous system that giving without scorekeeping won't destroy you. Start small: buy a stranger's coffee, send a voice note to someone you've been withholding from, offer help you won't track.
