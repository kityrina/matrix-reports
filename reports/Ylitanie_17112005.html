The shadow risk here is attracting partners who want you for the brand — the heat, the image, the social currency — and mistaking that for love. That drains you and keeps you small. The move: choose partners who see your *capacity*, not just your charisma. When love feels like a place to rest instead of a stage to perform on, the money follows.

(11) Strength

Strength guides you toward calm power — the kind that doesn't need to prove itself, that wins through presence, not force.

This energy lives in your body and in how you hold a room. It activates when you slow down instead of reacting, when you meet aggression with stillness, when you choose discipline over the easy dopamine hit. Physical practices anchor it — lifting, martial arts, anything that teaches you to respect your own body's intelligence. It also shows up in how you handle volatile people: you don't match their chaos, you contain it. The false-comfort version is performing strength. You talk louder, you push harder, you grip tighter — and it all backfires because real Strength never announces itself. Or you collapse entirely and let others steamroll you because holding your ground feels too confrontational. Neither works. The guidance here: soft spine, firm boundary. You don't need to roar, Ylitanie. The lion energy is already in you. Let it sit behind your eyes. That's enough.
