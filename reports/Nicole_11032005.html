Here's what works instead, babe: radical equality. Let people meet you on level ground — messy, uncertain, still figuring it out. 2. Notice the moment you start to "fix" someone in conversation and name it internally: "I'm doing the Overseer thing." Then physically lean back, stay quiet, and let them finish their own thought. Practice this five times this week. The silence will feel excruciating. Sit in it. Most people don't need your solution; they need your presence without the performance.

3. Journal on this prompt every morning for 21 days: "Where did I avoid being seen as equal yesterday?" Write three examples. One will be obvious (you gave advice). One will be subtle (you stayed calm while someone else cried). One will surprise you. The pattern reveals itself through repetition, not insight.

The 10 (Wheel of Fortune) here gives you natural timing and the ability to read a room without effort. Vision stays sharp, intuition flows, you catch patterns others miss. But when you're spinning in indecision or waiting for permission from the universe instead of acting, this centre stalls. Watch for eye strain, sinus pressure, brain fog that won't lift. The karmic tail — your 21 (The World) from The Overseer pattern — sits here too, Nicole. Past-life arrogance left a residue: you can see the whole board but doubt you're allowed to play. Trust what you see. Move on it.

Throat (Vishuddha) · 17 — The Star

Body systems: Thyroid gland, trachea, bronchi, throat, vocal cords, shoulders, arms, seventh cervical vertebra, all cervical vertebrae, lower jaw, lower jaw teeth.

The 17 (Star) makes you a natural communicator when you're not dimming yourself. Clear voice, easy visibility, words that land without effort. Thyroid stays balanced, neck and shoulders stay loose. The trap is performing hope you don't feel or going silent to avoid rocking the boat. Then the throat constricts: recurring sore throats, tightness in the neck, voice that cracks under stress, thyroid imbalance. You're meant to be seen and heard, but only when it's true. Fake-shining corrodes this channel faster than silence. Speak or don't — just don't perform either one.

Heart (Anahata) · 5 — The Hierophant

Body systems: Heart, circulatory system, respiratory system, lungs, bronchi, thoracic spine, ribs, shoulder scapular area, chest.

Your 5 (Hierophant) and 15 (The Devil) stack here — tradition meeting raw desire, mentorship crossing into magnetic pull. This is the zone of deep feeling and dangerous loyalty. When you're honouring both structure and passion, the heart stays open: strong circulation, regulated nervous system, generous without depletion. When you betray either pole — staying in dead structures or chasing heat without integrity — the heart protests. Palpitations, chest tightness, blood pressure swings, that feeling of a weight sitting on your sternum. The female line's 15 runs through here too. Your mother's unexpressed desire lives in your chest until you claim yours cleanly.

Solar Plexus (Manipura) · 6 — The Lovers

Body systems: Gastrointestinal tract, abdominal organs, pancreas, spleen, liver, gallbladder, small intestine, central part of the spine.

The 6 (Lovers) governs this centre — your core identity, your capacity to choose from values instead of fear. When you're clear on what you want and who you are, digestion is smooth, energy is steady, confidence is quiet. When you're people-pleasing or stuck in paralysis between two paths, the solar plexus knots. Stomach issues, blood sugar crashes, that anxious flutter that lives just under the ribs, chronic fatigue that no amount of sleep fixes. This is your centre of destiny showing up in the body. The cure is decisive action aligned with your actual values, not the ones you inherited.

Sacral (Svadhisthana) · 13 — Death (Rebirth)

Body systems: Adrenal glands, uterus and ovaries, kidneys, intestines, prostate gland in men, lumbar spinal column.

The 13 (Death) here is the heaviest load on your physical map. This is the womb, the hips, the lower back, the creative and sexual core. Transformation is your nature in this zone, but forced or resisted change lands here as pain. Reproductive issues, menstrual irregularities, lower back that seizes when you're holding on too tight, pelvic tension. The karmic tail's 3 (Empress) from The Overseer sits here too — past-life control over creation now asks for surrender. You're meant to let things die and be reborn through you, not manage the process. When you try to white-knuckle transformation, the sacral screams. Let go before it forces you.

Root (Muladhara) · 7 — The Chariot

Body systems: Urogenital system, lower limbs, large intestine, tailbone, sacrum, legs.

The 7 (Chariot) at the root gives you natural momentum and strong legs to carry ambition. When you're moving toward what's true, this centre is solid: stable energy, strong bones, grounded presence, healthy adrenals. The danger is bulldozing when you should pause or pushing expansion for its own sake. Then the root destabilises: knee and ankle issues, adrenal burnout, chronic lower-body pain, that untethered feeling no amount of caffeine fixes. Your foundation is built on purposeful movement, not busy-ness. Rest isn't retreat. It's how the root recharges for the next real push.
