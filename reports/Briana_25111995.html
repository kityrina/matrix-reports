3. Choose one person or project and commit to six months of fidelity, no exits. Tell someone you trust what you're doing. Weekly check-ins. When it gets dull or hard, that's the curriculum — not a sign you picked wrong. You're learning that love isn't the honeymoon high; it's what happens when you stay after the high fades and build something that doesn't need constant novelty to survive.

The 17 (The Star) sits here, which means your intuition is aesthetic before it's verbal. You see the answer, you don't always hear it. This is a light channel — your third eye doesn't fog easily, and when your gut speaks it tends to be right in ways that are annoyingly obvious in retrospect. The danger isn't confusion, it's dismissing the knowing because it arrived too quietly or looked too beautiful to be serious. Eyestrain and sinus pressure show up when you override what you already saw three weeks ago. Trust the first flash. Your instinct doesn't need a committee vote.

Throat (Vishuddha) · 10 — The Wheel of Fortune

Body systems: Thyroid gland, trachea, bronchi, throat, vocal cords, shoulders, arms, seventh cervical vertebra, all cervical vertebrae, lower jaw, lower jaw teeth.

The 10 (Wheel of Fortune) is timing and cycles, which at the throat means you know *when* to say the thing almost better than what to say. Your communication runs on rhythm — there's a season for silence and a season for the speech, and you can feel the shift before it arrives. The shadow: swallowing words because the moment doesn't feel perfect yet, or talking in circles when the wheel has already turned and you missed it. Throat tightness, chronic cough, thyroid weirdness — all flags that you're holding something past its expiration date. Say it when the opening shows up, not when you've rehearsed it to death. The throat clears when you stop waiting for permission from the cosmos.

Heart (Anahata) · 13 — Death (Rebirth)

Body systems: Heart, circulatory system, respiratory system, lungs, bronchi, thoracic spine, ribs, shoulder scapular area, chest.

The 13 (Death) sits on your heart chakra, and this one we name directly. Transformation runs through your chest, which means your heart doesn't do static love — it does cycles of closeness and distance, death of old forms, rebirth into new shapes. The physical tells: heart palpitations, chest tightness, shallow breathing when you're clinging to a version of a relationship that's already over. Your body will force the ending if you won't. And here's the brutal mercy: your 15 (The Devil) from the karmic tail also touches this zone through the love line, so passion and release are knotted together for you. The heart heals when you let things end cleanly instead of dragging the corpse. Grieve fast, then move.

Solar Plexus (Manipura) · 3 — The Empress

Body systems: Gastrointestinal tract, abdominal organs, pancreas, spleen, liver, gallbladder, small intestine, central part of the spine.

The 3 (The Empress) lives here, your centre, your creation engine. This is where your abundance sits when it's not being strangled. Light channel — your gut is naturally generative, you make things, you feed people (literally or metaphorically), you overflow when conditions are right. The vulnerability is scarcity panic and the impulse to over-give until resentment builds. Digestive issues, nausea, that clenched feeling under the ribs — all signs you're mothering from an empty cup or trying to create in a space that doesn't want what you're offering. Your solar plexus opens when you give *from* fullness, not toward it. Feed yourself first. Non-negotiable.

Sacral (Svadhisthana) · 9 — The Hermit

Body systems: Adrenal glands, uterus and ovaries, kidneys, intestines, prostate gland in men, lumbar spinal column.

Double 9 (The Hermit) here — sacral physics and energy both. This is deep, withdrawn creative and sexual power that doesn't perform on demand. Your womb space (literal or energetic) needs solitude to recharge, and your sexuality is not group-sport energy. It's private, esoteric, slow to open. The 9 also anchors your karmic tail, Briana, which means the drag of loneliness-as-consequence runs right through this chakra. Reproductive issues, low libido, pelvic pain — watch for these when you're forcing intimacy or creating from obligation instead of genuine desire. Your sacral comes alive in chosen solitude and with partners who respect the hermit door. Let it be what it is.

Root (Muladhara) · 6 — The Lovers

Body systems: Urogenital system, lower limbs, large intestine, tailbone, sacrum, legs.

The 6 (The Lovers) sits at the root, choice and partnership as your foundation, which means your survival instinct is relational — you feel safest when the bond is clear. Light energy here, but the 6 is also your comet karma and the karmic tail's final number, so old betrayals echo. Lower back pain, leg issues, chronic fatigue — these flare when you're staying in a situation that requires you to lie about what you actually want. Your root stabilizes when your choices match your truth and the partnership is honest all the way down. Fake safety exhausts you. Real clarity, even if it's uncomfortable, lets the base breathe.
